Process & Working Together

How we work, and what to expect.

The simplest first step is filling in the contact form on our Contact page. If you'd rather talk it through before writing anything down, you can email enquiries@techcraft.eu to book a 30-minute discovery call. Alternatively, you can use our AI project consultant Craft to describe what you're after and generate a project brief automatically — no technical vocabulary required.

Timelines vary by scope. Brand identity projects typically run 4–6 weeks. A full web design and build is usually 8–14 weeks depending on complexity. Campaign creative can move much faster — often 2–4 weeks — because the creative scope is narrower. You'll receive a detailed timeline as part of the scoping document we send after the discovery call, before any work begins.

Every project has a dedicated account lead who manages communication and keeps things on track. Depending on the scope, you'll also interact directly with the relevant specialist — Creative Director, Head of Engineering or ML & Data Lead — during review sessions. We don't use account managers as a buffer between you and the people doing the work.

Standard packages include two structured revision rounds — one after initial concepts and one after the refined designs. We collect all feedback in a single consolidated pass at each stage rather than handling it piecemeal, which keeps momentum and prevents scope creep. Work that falls outside those two rounds is scoped and costed separately on request.

Yes — nearly all of our project work is delivered remotely. We've worked with clients across Africa, Europe and North America. All reviews, handoffs and approvals happen digitally. For calls and presentations, we work around time zone differences and always confirm scheduling in advance so nothing feels rushed.

Scope changes happen, and we handle them directly. If something outside the original brief comes up, your account lead will flag it, explain the impact on timeline and cost, and you approve or decline before anything is actioned. We never add scope without client sign-off, and we never surprise you with an invoice for work you didn't knowingly request.
Technology & Tools

ML Insights, Craft AI and what's under the hood.

The ML Insights Engine is TechCraft's proprietary post-launch reporting system. It embeds lightweight behavioural tracking into every site we build — capturing click patterns, scroll depth, dwell time, exit signals and sentiment indicators. A machine learning model processes this data weekly and generates a plain-English report delivered to your client dashboard: what's working, what isn't, and what to prioritise. It's included by default in all web and product design projects, and available as an add-on for campaign landing pages.

Craft is an AI chatbot available inside the TechCraft client portal. In the pre-project phase, it helps you articulate what you want — even in plain, non-technical language — by asking guided questions about your audience, goals, budget range and timeline, then generating a structured project brief automatically. After launch, Craft reads your ML Insights reports and translates the data into specific, actionable recommendations: "Your mobile hero section has a 68% scroll-past rate — the headline is likely too long."

Our tracking collects anonymous, aggregated behavioural signals — no personally identifiable information is captured or stored. Data is processed in aggregate to identify patterns across sessions, not individuals. We implement a standard cookie consent banner on all sites we build, and the tracking only activates after user consent is granted. We provide a data processing addendum and full technical documentation on request.

No. The entire system is designed to be useful to non-technical people. Reports are written in plain English with clear recommendations, not raw data tables or analyst jargon. If a recommendation requires a design or development change, you can request that work from TechCraft directly and we'll scope it quickly. The goal is to give you the clarity of a data team without needing to hire one.

Yes. Our ML tracking layer can push data to a range of CRM, marketing automation and analytics platforms via webhook or API. Common integrations include HubSpot, Salesforce, GA4 and Segment. Custom integrations are assessed on a case-by-case basis and scoped as part of the project build phase. Let us know what's in your stack early and we'll flag any constraints upfront.
Pricing & Engagement

Budgets, contracts and what comes after launch.

We don't publish fixed price lists because every project is different in scope, complexity and timeline. What we do publish are indicative budget ranges in our scoping documents. Brand identity projects typically range from $8,000–$30,000. Web and product design from $15,000–$60,000. Campaigns from $5,000–$25,000. The discovery call gives us what we need to quote accurately before you commit to anything.

Standard projects are invoiced in three stages: 40% at project kick-off, 40% at design approval (before build begins), and 20% at final delivery. For larger or longer engagements, we can arrange milestone-based payment schedules that spread the cost across the project timeline. All payment terms are laid out in the project agreement before work starts.

Yes. We offer monthly retainers covering continued ML reporting and interpretation, design and content updates, Craft access for ongoing brief generation, and priority response times. Retainer clients also get quarterly strategy reviews and early access to new TechCraft features. Retainer packages start from $2,500/month and are discussed at the project handover session.

If a client needs to pause or cancel a project, work completed to that point is invoiced at the agreed day rate and any deposits covering future work are returned pro-rata. We ask for a minimum two-week notice for pauses and four weeks for full cancellations. Full terms are set out in the project agreement, which we send before any work begins. We've never had a client walk away mid-project, but we want the process to be fair if it ever happens.

Full intellectual property rights transfer to you on receipt of final payment. This includes all source files, design assets, code repositories and any custom typeface licences procured for your project. We retain the right to display the work in our portfolio and case study materials, which you can restrict by request at the time of signing.
